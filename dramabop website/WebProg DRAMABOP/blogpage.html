Kimi to Sekai ga Owaru Hi ni (2021)

While waiting for the episodes of the ongoing hit Korean drama, Happiness,
I found this drama in the recommendations on MyDramalist.

The drama has two seasons which have been aired and I shall talk about both of them.

Cast

Ryoma Takeuchi as Hibiki Mamiya
Ayami Nakajo as Kurumi Ogasawara
Kasamatsu Sho as Hiro Todoroki
Marie Iitoyo as Kanae Hiiragi

Summary for season 1

Mamiya Hibiki is a brilliant young man with a true heart. He lives with his partner Ogasawara Kurumi and
works as an auto mechanic. They've been dating since they were in high school. Hibiki devises a strategy
for proposing to Kurumi. He slides off a tunnel and becomes imprisoned on the day he plans to propose to
his sweetheart. He is able to flee four days later, but he is thrust into a new world. There are no
people outside, the traffic system is broken, and the streets are strewn with blood.
What happened to the rest of the world? Hibiki searches for Kurumi.

Views on Season 1

The first episode or so did not impress me, but the subsequent episodes became more powerful and
engaging. The writers did an excellent job of capturing the depths to which individuals descend
in their attempts to live, as well as moral difficulties, how people are valued differently in our
society due to variables they cannot control, and how big groups of people may be easily influenced.

To be honest, the zombies weren't the most terrifying aspect of the story; the other survivors were.
Initially, the drama never failed to deliver on the thrills, tensions, and horror that it promised.

The second half of the drama lost its charm a bit as the focus shifted to the romance aspect and
not in the way I was hoping.But it was still entertaining to watch

Summary for season 2

As a new community "House of Hope" has appeared. Who are they with a prosperous civilization?
In addition, a golem with an "x" that seems to have been artificially attached appears.
A new threat comes to those who survive the "End-of-world".

Views on season 2

In this season, our hero lost quite a bit of his charm and was not even there for two entire episodes. Kurumi really pissed me off this season but with reason.

The true show stealers were Kanae and Todoroki who have grown into much better individuals during this
time of peril. They are still flawed characters but they try their best to survive. I truly hope the
upcoming season 3 gives these two more screentime and gives us more of their story.

Season 2 just had 6 episodes, which I am actually glad about since the plot
kind of spiraled downward but it left me wanting another season for sure.
